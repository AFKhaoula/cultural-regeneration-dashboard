# Problem Statement: Cultural Regeneration Dashboard

## Executive Summary

City planners and heritage investment officers in postindustrial port cities lack a **data-driven decision support tool** to evaluate maritime heritage regeneration opportunities and optimize creative economy development investments. This results in:
- A lot of funding to sites with low economic potential
- Missed opportunities to attract high growth creative industries
- Lacking of strategic heritage preservation decisions

The **Cultural Regeneration Dashboard** addresses this gap by providing integrated analytics on site performance, economic impact, and cross-regional comparisons and evidence-based investment prioritization startegies.

---

## The Problem

### **Context: Post-Industrial Maritime Cities Face a Dilemma**

Cities like Glasgow, Liverpool, Rotterdam, and Hamburg possess **valuable maritime heritage assets** (shipyards, docks, warehouses, cranes) that are:
- Culturally significant 
- Abandoned or underutilized
- Expensive to maintain and redevelop
- Uncertain ROI on regeneration investments

**The Question Decision-Makers Face:**  
*"Which heritage sites should we invest in, what industries should we target, and how do we measure such success?"*

### **Current State: **

Today, heritage investment decisions are based on:
- **Copying blindly what worked in other places** 
- **Consultant reports not up to date** 
- **Gut feeling**

**What's Missing:**
- Real-time economic performance tracking
- Cross-site comparison capabilities
- Industry clustering analysis
- Trend visibility
- Regional benchmarking (Europe vs. emerging markets)
- Predictive indicators of success

### **The Stakes**

Poor regeneration decisions cost cities:
- **Huge sums in public investment
- **Years of delayed economic benefit
- **Lost creative industry jobs** that relocate elsewhere
- **Community displacement** 
- **Heritage loss and deterioration**

## Who Experiences This Problem?

### **Primary Users**

**1. Heritage Investment Officers**  
- **Role:** Allocate regeneration funding 
- **Pain Point:** "I need to justify why Site A gets X amount of investment instead of Site B"
- **Current Workaround:** Commission expensive consultant reports (in time and in money)
- **What They Need:** Real-time site comparison with ROI projections

**2. Urban Development Directors**  
- **Role:** Set city-wide regeneration strategy
- **Pain Point:** "Which creative industries should we recruit? What infrastructure do they need?"
- **Current Workaround:** Copy what other cities did 
- **What They Need:** Industry clustering analysis + regional best practices

**3. Economic Development Agencies**  
- **Role:** Attract creative businesses to heritage sites
- **Pain Point:** "We market to everyone and attract no one"
- **Current Workaround:** Generic "come to our city" campaigns
- **What They Need:** Data on which industries thrive at which site types

### **Secondary Users**

**4. City Councilors**  
- **Need:** Communicate ROI to citizens
- **Current Gap:** Vague promises, no measurable outcomes

**5. Cultural Organizations**  
- **Need:** Understand visitor patterns to optimize programming
- **Current Gap:** No general/global view

**6. Real Estate Developers**  
- **Need:** Assess commercial viability of heritage site projects
- **Current Gap:** No standardized benchmarks 

---

## Why Does This Problem Exist?

### **1. Data Fragmentation**
- Maritime heritage data sits in archives
- Economic impact tracked by no one
- Market comparisons require **Trade Ministry reports**
- **Result:** No one has the full picture

### **2. Legacy Processes**
- Decisions still based on manual reports
- Updates happen **annually** 
- No culture of **continuous real-time measurement**

### **3. Lack of Domain-Specific Tools**
- No pre-built dashboards for heritage regeneration use case
- Consultants build custom reports that can't be updated

### **4. No Cross-Regional Learning**
- Each city treats regeneration as unique
- Success patterns in Rotterdam aren't visible to Glasgow planners

## Constraints & Considerations

### **Data Limitations**
- **Sample data** used for MVP is fictional
- **Real deployment** would require integrating with:
  - City planning databases
  - Business registry APIs
  - Tourism/visitor tracking systems
  - International trade statistics

### **Political Sensitivities**
- Heritage decisions are politically charged (neighborhood interests, preservation vs. development debates)
- Dashboard must present **data objectively** without appearing to favor specific sites
- Recommendations must be defensible with clear methodology

### **Change Management**
- Users accustomed to consultant-led decision-making
- Need training on self-service analytics
- Requires cultural shift toward "data-informed" from "experience-based" decisions

## Current Alternatives & Why They Fall Short

### **Alternative 1: Hire Consultants**
- Deep expertise
- high price per engagement
- 6-12 month turnaround
- Outdated the moment delivered
- Can't be updated by internal team

### **Alternative 2: Build Custom BI Reports**
- Tailored to needs
- Requires data team (3+ people)
- Not user-friendly for non-technical planners

### **Alternative 3: Excel Spreadsheets**
- Familiar tool
- Can't handle 4-table relational data
- No visualization capabilities
- Can't scale beyond 1-2 sites


**Our Solution:**  
Self-service + real-time + low cost + scalable + user-friendly
